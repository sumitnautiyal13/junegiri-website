Five commitments

How we actually run the place.

Not buzzwords. Specific operational choices we've made over the last four years.

☀️

1. Energy & water

Sun heats every drop of water on property. We catch and store enough rainwater each monsoon to handle our garden and kitchen needs through the dry months.

  • 100% solar-heated water (all rooms + kitchen)
  • Rooftop solar covers 40% of electrical load
  • Rainwater harvesting tank: 25,000 litres
  • LED-only lighting throughout property
  • Greywater recycled for garden irrigation
♻️

2. Zero single-use plastic

Every guest gets a refillable steel bottle on arrival. Refill stations are everywhere. We haven't bought packaged water bottles in 3 years.

  • Steel water bottles for every guest
  • Reverse-osmosis drinking water station
  • Bulk-bought toiletries in glass dispensers
  • Cloth bags for laundry, not plastic
  • Compostable straws & take-away packaging
🌾

3. Farm-to-table kitchen

Most of what you eat on a 3-day stay came from within 2 km. The rest comes from named farms within Yamkeshwar block.

  • On-site organic vegetable garden (0.4 acre)
  • Atta, dal, ghee from local Pahadi farmers
  • No imported ingredients · no packaged sauces
  • Compost from kitchen + garden waste
  • Recipes from Geetu's grandmother's notebook
🪵

4. Construction & materials

JuneGiri was built using locally-quarried stone and reclaimed Himalayan pine. We didn't ship in marble or concrete from elsewhere.

  • Locally-quarried stone walls
  • Reclaimed pine from old village houses
  • Lime-mud plaster (not cement) in 60% of rooms
  • Slate roofing — locally sourced
  • Furniture handmade in Pauri district
👥

5. People & community

The point of running a Pahadi homestay is to keep Pahadi families employed in Pahad. We don't bring staff from outside.

  • 80% of staff hired from villages within 10 km
  • Equal pay across gender
  • Provident fund & ESI for all permanent staff
  • Daughters of staff: schooling support fund
  • Annual rural school art programme (2022→)
🐾

6. Wildlife & forest

We share the property with Rajaji's elephants, leopards, and 300+ bird species. We've designed the site to minimise our footprint on their habitat.

  • No fence-cutting · forest trails follow animal paths
  • Lights-out by 10:30 PM (sky-darkening)
  • Sound below 50 dB after 9 PM
  • Bird-safe glass on all balcony windows
  • Quarterly wildlife survey with Rajaji forest dept.
