Our Story & Founders

Geetu & Vignesh.
Two people who chose the hills.

JuneGiri was born from a deeply personal journey. Geeta Payal, co-founder of Junegiri Farms, holds a Bachelor of Arts from Delhi University and trained as a fashion designer — a corporate career she eventually left behind to pursue a life rooted in nature, wellness, and the mountains.

For more than a decade, she has been a yoga teacher and consultant — and that grounding philosophy shows up in everything we offer at JuneGiri Farms & Homestay.

Together, Geeta and her husband Vignesh — known warmly to guests simply as Geetu and Vignesh — chose to build their life and their business in Uttarakhand. They created JuneGiri Farms as a farm-stay experience nestled in the lush greenery of Rajaji National Park, a place where guests could genuinely connect with nature, eat home-cooked Pahadi food, and be welcomed like family.

"We didn't build a hotel. We built our home, and we open it to people who want what we wanted — quiet, forest, and a proper meal at the end of the day."

— Geetu

Located just 7 km from the famous Neelkanth Mahadev Temple in Rishikesh, the homestay sits in the middle of wilderness — yet feels like a true home away from home. From that heartfelt base, JuneGiri Yatra was created: to extend the same local knowledge, warmth, and authenticity into curated travel across Uttarakhand and the Himalayas.

Where we are

Inside the jungle.
7 km from the temple.

JuneGiri Farms sits in the village of Maral, Tehsil Yamkeshwar, in the Pauri Garhwal district of Uttarakhand — at the edge of Rajaji National Park.

We are 7 km from the famous Neelkanth Mahadev Temple and roughly 30–40 minutes from Laxman Jhula in Rishikesh. Far enough to feel the silence. Close enough to drive in for evening Aarti.

Wildlife is genuine here — elephants, leopards, deer, hundreds of bird species. The Ganga is a short drive away. The trails start at our gate.
